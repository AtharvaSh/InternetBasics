Web Services

Web Services are the software-based services that occur when two electronic devices connected via a network communicate over the World Wide Web's HTTP protocol.

Definitions

Downloading: Making a copy of a requested web page or file or document on your local computer.

Email: Stands for electronic mail. It is the most widely used method to send messages electronically on the internet.

Advantages of email: Low cost, speed, waste reduction, ease of use, record maintenance, patience.

Limitations of email: Hardware requirement, permanent storage, difficulties in conveying emotions.

Inbox: The name of the folder that stores your received mails.

Spam: Defined as irrelevant or inappropriate messages sent on the Internet to a large number of people.

Junk mail: The unwanted or unsolicited advertising or promotional material received through mail or email.

Search Engine: A program that searches through a database of web pages for particular information.

File Transfer Protocol (FTP): A commonly used protocol for exchanging files over any network that supports the TCP/IP protocol.

Telnet: An internet utility that lets you log on to remote computer systems.

Secure Shell (SSH): A remote login protocol that locks into a remote machine via a shell where all data between the client and server is encrypted.

Secure Copy Protocol: Allows you to transmit files from one machine to another over the internet with encryption benefits of SSH.

Secure File Transfer Protocol: A secure protocol for file transfer that ensures the security of data based on the Secure Shell protocol.

Chatting: Online textual talk.

Video Conferencing: A two-way video phone conversation among multiple participants.

e-Learning: Learning that takes place in an electronically simulated environment.

e-Banking: Availing banking services through electronic measures via the internet.

e-Shopping: The process of shopping done over the internet.

e-Reservation: Making reservations for tickets, hotel rooms, or tourist packages through the internet.

e-Governance: The application of electronic means in governance to fulfill the requirements of the common man at affordable costs and in the fastest possible time.

Online Committee: A group where people discuss topics of common interests on the internet.

Post: A message posted in an online discussion.

Social Networking: The use of internet-based social media programs to make connections with friends, family, classmates, customers, and clients.

Social Networking Site: A website that provides a venue for people to share their activities with family, friends, and colleagues or to share their interests on a particular topic.

Short Message Service (SMS): A text message that contains up to 160 characters.

Multimedia Messaging Service (MMS): A standard way to send messages that include multimedia content to and from mobile phones.
